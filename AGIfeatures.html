Key Features of Advanced AI

  • Conclusion Memory & Jump Thinking: AI should recall past conclusions dynamically and adapt thinking strategies.
  • Generalization from Few Examples: AI must extract patterns with minimal data.
  • Recall Trigger & Connection Recognition: Just like humans, AI should trigger relevant memories when recognizing patterns.
  • Self-Optimization & Knowledge Restructuring: The AI should evolve autonomously based on new data.
  • Self-Restructuring Architectures: Mechanisms like self-attention in transformers enable adaptive learning.
  • Probabilistic Reasoning: Bayesian models & uncertainty-aware transformers refine AI decision-making.
  • Reinforcement Learning: AI should adapt dynamically to complex environments.
  • Causal Understanding: AI must integrate causal inference models (e.g., Judea Pearl’s frameworks).
  • Symbolic-Connectionist Hybrid Models: Combining neural networks with abstract reasoning enhances intelligence.
  • Abstract Linking & Thought Optimization: Just as humans make intuitive leaps, AI should optimize processing efficiency while mitigating false knowledge.

AGI Research & Development

Researching AGI isn’t just about replicating specific human outputs. Instead, it's about replicating the totality of human cognitive abilities.

Human-like capabilities are essential—not just human-like outputs.

For example, LLMs can recall and answer questions, but they lack:

  • Real-time learning
  • Deep generalization from minimal examples
  • Efficient, structured reasoning
